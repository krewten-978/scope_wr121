## Grading Vocabulary for This Key

- **Persuasion:** what makes an audience believe, feel, or act.
- **Proof:** reasons and evidence that actually support a claim.
- **Logos / ethos / pathos:** reason-structure-evidence; speaker character/credibility; emotion.
- **Rhetorical force:** how powerfully a message lands, whether or not it proves.
- **Claim under proof:** the exact conclusion that still needs support.
- **Rhetoric-vs-proof map:** claim; audience/desired response; logos; ethos; pathos; proved; only suggested; force matches/exceeds/replaces proof.
- **Review terms:** Week 15 verbal/real/mixed dispute map; Week 10 missing-the-point / proof-to-claim match.

## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Blue out announcement (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Desired action/claim-pressure: wear blue, fill the gym, show up as champions. Audience: the student body. Main rhetorical force: pathos/identity pride and belonging (“if you care,” “champions”), not a proof that attendance causes championship success.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names action/claim, audience, and rhetorical force accurately
- 1 pt: Names two of the three elements
- 0 pts: Only restates the announcement

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Weekend work post (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Claim under proof: teachers who assign weekend work do not respect family time / are not “real educators.” Emotion/character pressure includes indignation, protective pathos about childhood, and ethos attack on teachers who assign weekend work.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: States the claim under proof and one clear pressure move
- 1 pt: Gives claim or pressure only
- 0 pts: Agrees or disagrees without analysis

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Drain bond speech (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Ethos: coached youth soccer for twelve years / local standing. Pathos: photos of flooded basements; child sleeping upstairs. Logos: vote for the drain bond as a means to reduce flooding/harm. Strong answers label all three without treating photos alone as full engineering proof.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Labels logos, ethos, and pathos accurately
- 1 pt: Labels two appeals clearly
- 0 pts: Only says the speech is emotional or persuasive

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Club recruitment (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Strongest appeal is often ethos/association or weak inductive logos (past presidents’ college results). Still needed before “join us if you are serious about your future” follows: whether the club caused those outcomes, whether the pattern will continue, whether coding/club work fits this freshman, and what “serious about your future” means.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names strongest appeal and what still needs proof
- 1 pt: One of those elements only
- 0 pts: Accepts the pitch as proof

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Halftime speech (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Rhetorically, the speech may increase energy, aggression, confidence, or team unity. It has not proved that the other team is soft and scared, nor that louder play will win. Force can exceed proof.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Separates possible rhetorical effect from unproved claim
- 1 pt: Mentions energy or unproved insult only
- 0 pts: Treats the speech as proof the other team is soft

## Question 6: Part 3 Q2 – Coding club flyer (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Supported smaller claim (if the data are real): the coding club was associated with higher average math scores last semester. Overreaching conclusion: every student should drop music for coding. The leap ignores other causes, student differences, the value of music, and whether correlation equals exclusive priority.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Separates limited support from overclaim
- 1 pt: Notices overclaim with thin explanation
- 0 pts: Accepts the “therefore every student” leap

## Question 7: Part 4 Q1 – Town meeting map (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Main claim: approve the cooling-center budget tonight. Audience/desired response: town meeting voters; immediate yes vote and moral solidarity. Logos: cracked bottle as heat evidence; consultants vs children contrast (partly suggestive). Ethos: grew up on the block; known family. Pathos: kids without air; paperwork-over-children charge. Proved or supported only weakly/local-sensory: heat hardship is vivid; speaker is local. Only suggested: consultants wasted money; delay equals choosing paperwork over children; tonight’s budget is the right remedy and amount. Force often exceeds or replaces full proof because applause and moral framing outrun budget evidence.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Completes a coherent map with force-vs-proof judgment
- 1 pt: Includes some map elements but incomplete
- 0 pts: Only summarizes the scene or cheers a side
