## Grading Vocabulary

- **Follow-test:** Decide whether the stated reason, together with any defensible hidden assumption, supports the conclusion.
- **Stable-term test:** Decide whether a key word keeps the same meaning throughout an argument.
- **Scope test:** Decide whether All, None, Some, or Most is no broader than the evidence supports.
- **Route consequence:** State what a solved logic problem permits the expedition to record about a door, including what remains unproved.
- **Relevant evidence:** Evidence that bears on the actual question: which passage is most likely to rise toward natural daylight.
- **Distractor:** A fact or object that attracts attention but does not help answer the route question.
- **Repair:** Revise a claim by stabilizing a term, narrowing its scope, or correcting its inference without abandoning its subject.
- **Carry-forward reasoning:** Score an earlier error where it occurs. In later synthesis, award credit for reasoning consistently from the student's recorded result while using the other evidence accurately; do not deduct twice for one misconception.
