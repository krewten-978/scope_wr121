## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Right to speak vs. right about policy (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
First **right**: a **claim or entitlement** (right to speak / legal or moral permission to speak).  
Second **right**: **correct / true** (right about the policy).

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Both distinct meanings identified
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Accept “legal right” vs. “factually correct.”

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Free country vs. free admission (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**

Second **free**: **without charge** / no admission fee.

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Both meanings identified
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Feathers are light (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **light**.  
Meanings: (1) **not heavy** / low weight (feathers are light); (2) **bright** / illuminated (what is light cannot be dark).  
Why it fails: The conclusion treats one sense as if it were the other; weight does not imply brightness.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term, both meanings, clear explanation
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Star in space vs. star as celebrity (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **star**.  
Meanings: (1) **celestial body** / ball of gas in space; (2) **famous performer** / celebrity (the lead singer is a star).  
Why it fails: Celebrity status does not make someone a physical star in space.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term, both meanings, clear failure explanation
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Fix end (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
First **end**: **goal / purpose / perfection** (the end of a thing is its perfection).  
Second **end**: **termination / conclusion** of life (death is the end of life).  
After fixing: The conclusion that **death is the perfection of life** does **not** follow — death as termination is not the same as life’s purpose or perfection.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Two fixed senses for *end* and correct “does not follow”
- 1 pt: Senses identified but weak follow judgment
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Classic “end of life” equivocation; accept “purpose” and “death/termination” language.

## Question 6: Part 4 Q1 – Nobody paragraph (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **nobody**.  
Senses: (1) **no one / not anybody** (“nobody is perfect” = no person is perfect); (2) **a person of no importance** (Jordan felt like a nobody).  
The flyer’s claim that Jordan “must be perfect” does **not** follow reliably — it equivocates by treating “nobody” in the universal sense as if it applied to Jordan as an insignificant person.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Both senses and explains the bad inference; may credit Jordan’s reply in the paragraph
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 7: Part 5 Q1 – Review: mayor tenure and lawful plan (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** The mayor’s new zoning plan must be lawful.  
**Stated reason:** The mayor has served in office longer than any other council member.  
**Hidden assumption:** Longest tenure implies lawful or correct policy.  
**Follows:** No.

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: All four elements; follows = no
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 9: Part 5 Q3 – Review: hiring paragraph (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** The finalist should be hired because proven test performance guarantees long-term success (paraphrase acceptable).  
**Stated reason:** Finalist answered every technical question correctly; no other candidate completed the same skills trial.  
**Hidden assumption:** Trial performance guarantees long-term job success.  
**Follows:** No or partly.  
**Distractors:** lunch, courtyard, cousin, meme.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Four elements; follows judged no/partly; distractors not treated as reasons
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing
