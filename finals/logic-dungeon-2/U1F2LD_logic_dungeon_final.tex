\section*{The Second Seal}

Beyond the Hall of Inscriptions, the expedition reaches a circular chamber. The next seal lies in the \textbf{Upper Archive}. Three routes leave the chamber:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=0.1em]
\item \textbf{Raven Stair} --- an upward arrow is cut beneath a raven seal.
\item \textbf{Well Passage} --- cold air flows through a round stone opening.
\item \textbf{Lantern Gallery} --- a blue survey thread disappears around its first turn.
\end{itemize}

Each route has an argument in its favor. A valid form may rest on a bad premise. A true conclusion may be supported by an invalid argument. The expedition must test both the bridge and the ground beneath it.

\cluebox{\textbf{Two-test rule:} First apply the \emph{form test}: if the premises were granted, would the conclusion follow? Then apply the \emph{fact test}: are the premises true, verified, doubtful, or false? The best route needs the strongest combination of form and facts.}

\vspace{0.3em}
\textbf{Tools available:} premises and conclusion; major, minor, and middle terms; practical syllogism map; A/E/I/O mood; figure; undistributed middle; two negative premises; validity; truth; soundness; premise pressure point.
