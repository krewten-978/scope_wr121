## Grading Vocabulary

- **Point at issue:** The exact question that must be answered for the proposed conclusion or action.
- **Nearby claim:** A related claim that evidence may support while leaving the actual point at issue unproved.
- **Partial proof:** Evidence that establishes only part of what the conclusion requires.
- **Repair:** Narrowing a conclusion to fit the evidence or adding evidence that bears on the actual issue.
- **Source case / target case:** The known comparison case and the case being judged by analogy.
- **Relevant similarity:** A likeness that bears on the proposed conclusion.
- **Disanalogy:** A difference that limits or defeats the proposed inference.
- **Overextension:** Making an analogy prove more than its relevant likeness supports.
- **Sample:** The cases actually examined.
- **Representative sample:** Cases varied and relevant enough to reflect the larger group or conditions being judged.
- **Counterexample:** A case that defeats a universal claim or requires it to be narrowed.
- **Overclaim / revised conclusion:** A claim broader than the evidence and a narrowed claim that fits the evidence.
- **Best-supported:** The option with the strongest relevant support among those available; not a guarantee.
- **Carry-forward rule:** Score an original error once. In later synthesis, award credit for accurate and coherent reasoning from the student's recorded earlier results, even when an earlier result was mistaken.
