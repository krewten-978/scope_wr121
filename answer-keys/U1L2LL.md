## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Two meanings of screen (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
One meaning: to **check / filter / review** (the IT office will screen the app).  
Second meaning: a **physical display surface** (welcome screen in the lobby).

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Both distinct meanings of *screen* identified correctly
- 0 pts: Incorrect, missing, or only one meaning

**Notes for grading:**  
- Accept close paraphrases (examine, test, monitor; monitor/display panel).

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Bright shifts meaning (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **bright**.  
Senses: (1) **intelligent / capable** (bright student); (2) **strong light / dazzling** (bright stage lights).

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Correct term and both senses
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- The absurd conclusion shows the equivocation; students need not restate the conclusion.

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Cold patient and cold song (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **cold**.  
Meanings: (1) **low temperature / chilly conditions** (patients left in the cold); (2) **emotionally distant / unfeeling style** (a cold song).  
Why it fails: The conclusion about patients and the song does not follow because the two senses of *cold* are unrelated; the argument only “works” by equivocating.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term, both meanings, and clear explanation of failure
- 1 pt: Term and meanings correct but weak explanation
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Reward explicit mention of equivocation or “same word, two ideas.”

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Open democracy and open gate (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **open**.  
Meanings: (1) **free public discussion / transparency** (open debate in democracy); (2) **physically unlocked or not closed** (gate left open).  
Why it fails: Physical openness of a gate is not the same as democratic openness; the conclusion equivocates.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term, both meanings, clear failure explanation
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Fix equal (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
First *equal*: **fair / same rules and opportunities for everyone** (equality requires fair rules).  
Second *equal*: **same measurement** (twins equal in height).  
After fixing: The conclusion that fair rules require everyone to be the **same height** does **not** follow — fair rules do not require identical physical traits.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Two distinct fixed meanings for *equal* and correct “does not follow” (or equivalent)
- 1 pt: Meanings fixed but weak follow judgment
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Full credit does not require Whately vocabulary; require consistent senses and a fair judgment.

## Question 6: Part 4 Q1 – Interest in the paragraph (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
Shifting term: **interest** (and related phrase **public interest**).  
Typical senses: (1) **attention / engagement** (held the community’s interest; lost interest in meetings); (2) **public welfare / common good** (protect the public interest); (3) **money earned on savings / financial return** (interest on savings accounts).  
The final conclusion equivocates **public interest** (civic welfare) with **interest on savings** (financial). The conclusion is unreliable because the committee blends unrelated senses of *interest*.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Identifies *interest* as the problem, at least two relevant senses, explains unreliable conclusion; ignores jazz/lunch/meme as main logic
- 1 pt: Partial senses or treats a distractor as the core shift
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Accept two-sense answers if student clearly links public-good vs financial interest.

## Question 9: Part 5 Q3 – Review: hiring paragraph (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** The finalist should be hired because proven test performance guarantees long-term success in the role (accept paraphrase of the official note).
