## Grading Vocabulary

- **Minor term (S):** Subject of the conclusion.
- **Major term (P):** Predicate of the conclusion.
- **Middle term (M):** Term appearing in both premises but not the conclusion; it is supposed to connect S and P.
- **Major premise:** Premise containing the major term.
- **Minor premise:** Premise containing the minor term.
- **Mood:** A/E/I/O pattern in major premise, minor premise, conclusion order.
- **Figure:** Placement of M in the premises. Figure 1 is M–P / S–M. Figure 2 is P–M / S–M.
- **Form test:** If the premises were granted, would the conclusion have to follow?
- **Fact test:** Are the premises true, verified, doubtful, or false?
- **Undistributed middle:** M never refers to its whole class, so sharing M does not force S and P together. Beginner pattern: All P are M; All S are M; therefore All S are P.
- **Pressure point:** Premise most in need of factual proof and most important to the argument's success.
- **Valid:** The conclusion follows from the premises because of form.
- **Sound:** Valid form plus true/established premises.
- **Not yet proved / valid but doubtful:** The form is valid, but at least one needed premise lacks enough factual support.
- **Invalid with a true observation:** A premise may accurately report a fact, but the form still fails to prove the conclusion.
- **Carry-forward reasoning:** Score an earlier error where it occurs. In later synthesis, award credit when the student reasons consistently from a recorded result while handling the remaining evidence accurately; do not deduct twice for one misconception.
