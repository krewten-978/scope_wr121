## 1. Where the company is now

### Physical sequence already established

1. **Hall of Inscriptions** — first underground approach; doors, sample ledgers, false daylight signs.
2. **Surveyor's Rotunda** — circular junction; Raven, Well, Lantern, Rootbridge.
3. **Rootbridge Path** — best-supported safe route under mason and maintenance records.
4. **Upper Archive** — destination of Dungeon II; original seals and higher records of the fortress complex.
5. **Court of the Oracle** — a **working chamber of the Upper Archive complex**, built into the cliff-side levels of *Castrum Malae Ratiocinationis*.

The Court is reached after the company has already paid for entry with careful form-and-fact work. The next danger is not “can we get upstairs?” but “once we are among the sealed records, who gets to **speak with authority** about what they mean?”

### First impression of the Upper Archive

The Upper Archive is not a single reading room. It is a stacked complex of:

- seal vaults and document niches;
- a surveyors’ mezzanine (matching Rotunda maps);
- a maintenance gallery with older cistern access notes;
- a formal **Court chamber** cut into living rock, facing an inner wall of three identical-format niches.

The air is cooler and drier than Rootbridge. Dust is fine. Lamp-soot streaks show many prior visitors. Some seals are cracked; some are intact; some were clearly opened and resealed by different hands at different times. A brass plaque near the Court entrance still reads something in the spirit of: **Rulings of the Court are final for the present business of the Archive.** (Final for *business* — not necessarily true about the world.)

Nothing here needs monsters. The danger is **authoritative procedure**.

## 2. What the Oracles are (institutional)

### Core definition

In this complex, an Oracle is **not** a prophet and **not** a ghost.

An Oracle is a **permanent bureaucratic office** installed as a niche-and-tablet decision engine. Builders and later administrators used it when a decision had to be **issued with public finality** even though supporting records were incomplete, conflicting, or technical.

In short:

- The fortress required **rulings** (pass, seal, open, forbid, authorize a route, declare a person trustworthy).
- Ordinary records often could not settle those rulings cleanly.
- The Oracle system produces **settled speech** — answers that *sound* finished because they have the correct form, stamp, and counter-stamp.

That is why the Court fits Weeks 7–9. The problem of the Court is **apparent proof dressed as procedure**.

### How the machine works (no magic)

1. A question is written on a **Petition Strip** and placed in a niche tray.
2. The niche’s sitting officer (the interpreter of that office) draws or letters a **Reply Tablet** from that niche’s stock of formula language and current “standing orders.”
3. The reply is announced at the **Interpreters’ Bench**.
4. An **Echo Tablet** may restate the reply in larger, more confident language so the company hears one clear command.

Stock phrases, restamped seals, and “confirmation by restatement” are the whole show. There is no second, invisible power behind the niches. The enraging part is that **everyone treats the finished form as if it had done the work of proof**.

### Three Oracles = three characters

Student-facing world should present **three Oracles**, each with:

- a niche;
- a jurisdiction;
- a sitting officer who *is* the Oracle’s public face;
- a tone of bureaucratic personality;
- a preferred kind of finished speech.

They should feel like three officials who share a room and hate yielding jurisdiction — not like three props with name tags.

## 3. Who put them there (approved history)

**Layer A — Original fortress architects (deep past)**  
Needed orderly command under incomplete knowledge. Built the Court and three niches as instruments of governance, not entertainment.

**Layer B — Imperial Archive Authority (middle past)**  
Reused the Court as a **records tribunal**. Interpreters trained as **phrase-makers of finality**. Explains dated cards, seals, professional tone that can still be obsolete or circular.

**Layer C — Later clerks and stranded expeditions (recent past)**  
Panic glosses, loyalty language, synonym restatements, restamped “binding” marks after loss of original rings. The Court still runs because bureaucracy outlives the people who understood its limits.

World truth for designers: the fortress favors the *form* of settled judgment. That is one of its defenses.

## 4. Character dossiers: the three Oracles

Each Oracle is a **character-office**. The niche is the desk; the sitting officer is the person students meet.

### Oracle I — Passage  
**Public face:** **Master of Ways** (sitting officer of the Passage niche)  
**Jurisdiction:** routes, doors, whether a company may advance  
**Surface appeal:** technical, competent, survey-trained; sounds like the person who “actually knows the map”  
**Bureaucratic personality:** precise, slightly bored superiority; treats disagreement as navigational illiteracy  
**Physical tells:** blue thread tags on sleeve (echo of Lantern Gallery professionalism); route stamps; a folding rule  
**Early pitch to the company:** there is a **ceremonially correct inner door** (or stair) that Passage has already authorized; the company should take it because Passage has declared the way *clear* / *open* / *sound*  
**World pressure point:** Passage’s strongest-looking words are ordinary bureaucratic terms that can shift mid-argument (*sound*, *clear*, *open*, *free*, *in order*)  
**What Passage actually has early:** some true local observations (a draft, a dry threshold, an unbroken stamp on a first landing) that do **not** yet prove full-company advance  
**Late fate under full evidence:** form of authority intact; independent support for full-company advance fails or remains incomplete; diagnosis stays **provisional / not authorized for all twenty**

### Oracle II — Seal  
**Public face:** **Keeper of Marks** (sitting officer of the Seal niche)  
**Jurisdiction:** documents, authenticity, which record “counts” as binding  
**Surface appeal:** meticulous, archival, uncorruptible-looking; the person who “won’t move without proper paper”  
**Bureaucratic personality:** cold courtesy; treats unstamped caution as chaos  
**Physical tells:** seal-ring on a chain; ledger of “current binding marks”; wax chips on the cuff  
**Early pitch to the company:** a **packet** (or door warrant) is binding because it bears the Archive’s authentic mark; therefore the company must treat that packet’s instruction as settled  
**World pressure point:** authentication that **assumes what it must prove** — “binding because properly sealed / properly sealed because binding Archive issue”; loaded definitions of *authentic*, *Archive-true*, *current*  
**What Seal actually has early:** real stamps, real trays, real procedure — and at least one **restamped** or **reissued** mark whose physical history is incomplete  
**Late fate under full evidence:** circular or question-begging chain exposed by Warden/seal-cabinet notes (restamp dates, lost original ring, “do not treat restamp as load-tested authority”); packet may still be historically interesting without being full-company authorization

### Oracle III — Charge  
**Public face:** **Advocate of the Bench** (sitting officer of the Charge niche)  
**Jurisdiction:** duty, loyalty, courage, what a “true” member of the company / Archive order must accept  
**Surface appeal:** moral urgency; sounds like leadership under pressure  
**Bureaucratic personality:** warm threat; wraps disagreement in identity and shame while still using official verbs (*must*, *shall*, *is required*)  
**Physical tells:** clean collar; Echo Tablet mallet near hand; list of “standing charges” in a black folder  
**Early pitch to the company:** hesitation is disloyalty; a *true courier* / *real supporter of the Archive* accepts the Court ruling without further proof; silence or delay proves unfitness  
**World pressure point:** **appearance of proof** by emotional and rhetorical force; question-begging epithets; restatement of duty as if restatement were evidence  
**What Charge actually has early:** real social force in a closed room; possibly one true observation about risk of delay — used to leap to a verdict about character  
**Late fate under full evidence:** powerful as pressure, weak as proof; cannot authorize a route or a seal by shame alone

### How they feel like a trio (not three worksheets)

They interrupt each other. Each claims **exclusive jurisdiction** over part of the company’s next act:

- Passage: “Ways decide movement.”  
- Seal: “Nothing moves without a mark.”  
- Charge: “A company that will not accept a finished ruling is not a company.”  

They agree on one bureaucratic dogma: **a finished Court ruling is the proper end of inquiry.**  
They disagree on which niche’s finish is the real finish. That mutual certainty is the room’s weather.

### Secondary office (not a fourth Oracle)

**Niche Warden / Records Service** — not an Oracle. Maintenance and records-service clerk for the physical Court: humidity, tablet restocking, seal-cabinet repairs, service corridors. Tone: tired, factual, under-ranked. Early, the three Oracles treat the Warden as **out of order** (wrong desk, no jurisdiction to authorize). Late, the Warden’s ledgers and the **Records Service Corridor** become the independent path.

**Bench Scribe** (optional thin presence): letters Echo Tablets; restates conclusions as “confirmations.” Can appear as a document rather than a speaking character if page space is tight.

## 5. What the Court is doing when the party arrives

Mid-procedure, fully institutional:

- A petition is already open: **how may a full company of twenty proceed from the Court deeper into the Archive complex?**
- All three Oracles have issued or are issuing **Reply Tablets**.
- An **Echo Tablet** may already be repeating one of them in larger letters.
- The **ceremonial inner door** has three empty seal-cups (one under each Oracle). Custom says a proper advance fills the cups and opens under Court finish.
- Behind a dusty hanging: a grated **Records Service Corridor** used by clerks between Court and the working stacks. No seal-cups. No Echo. Looks improper.

The Oracles are not predicting futures. They are doing their job: **manufacturing finality**.

## 6. The choice architecture (world + puzzle)

### Question the company must answer

**Which authorization, if any, is best supported as the next step for all twenty?**

Options that exist in the room:

| Option | Early surface | Early logical weather | Late status (locked outcome) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Passage’s way** (ceremonial advance under Master of Ways) | Professional, map-smart | Looks strong; word-shift risk | **Problematic** — true scraps, no independent full-company authorization |
| **Seal’s packet** (obey the “binding” warrant) | Proper paper | Looks strongest to rule-followers | **Problematic** — circular/restamped authority exposed |
| **Charge’s demand** (accept without further proof as loyalty) | Moral heat | Feels decisive in a crowded room | **Problematic** — appearance of proof / begging the question |
| **Records Service Corridor** (Warden / clerks’ working route) | Ugly, irregular, “not a ruling” | Early: dismissed; warnings look like rejection | **Best-supported next step under recovered service records** — limited, inspectable, non-ceremonial advance for the company |

### Why this winner (designer rationale)

Mirrors Dungeon II without copying stairs:

- Early field is **clogged with finished-looking speech**.
- Every ceremonial option has a **real institutional virtue** (technical skill, paperwork care, moral seriousness) that makes rejection feel costly.
- The service corridor looks like **procedural noncompliance** — exactly what bureaucracy trains people to fear.
- Late evidence should be **physical and clerical**, not a last-page prophecy: service inspection dates, corridor load notes, seal-cabinet restamp history, Warden’s tablet-stock list showing which “replies” are stock formulas.
- Culminating language for eventual student text: **best-supported next step**, not guaranteed safety and not “the Oracles are fake so anything goes.”

### Delayed-field rule (must survive into the test)

1. Stations 1–early middle: each Oracle’s case can be diagnosed, but **none** may be safely accepted for all twenty; service corridor remains unproved or wrongly discarded.  
2. Middle records: undermine ceremonial certainty (restamps, stock phrases, word-shifts, loyalty circles) without yet fully proving the corridor.  
3. Late records: establish independent support for the service corridor as a workable full-company passage into the next archive level **and** clarify what the corridor does *not* prove (it is not a ceremonial victory, not proof of every later gallery, not a substitute for later evidence work in Dungeon IV).  
4. Final chart/decision: students combine diagnoses; the answer should not be available from atmosphere alone.

If a draft makes Passage, Seal, or Charge correct early, rewrite the world evidence — do not rename the winner.

3. **Echo Tablet frame** — wall slot; restatement-as-confirmation machine.

8. **Records Service Corridor grate** — dusty hanging, no cups, clerks’ working route toward later stacks (Dungeon IV seed).

## 8. What “False Oracle” means here

Not “every sentence is a lie.”  
**False** = counterfeit *office of proof*: speech that wears procedure’s marks without independent support.

Some stamps are historically real. Some observations are true. Some duties are morally serious. The defect is often in the **join** between mark and conclusion — the bureaucratic move that treats **finished form** as **earned proof**.

Whately’s limit holds: logic tests whether support reaches the claim. It does not by itself invent corridor measurements, seal provenance, or moral worth. Those need records, observation, and judgment.

### Into Dungeon IV — *Archive of Broken Proofs*
The service corridor should deliver the company into **working stacks**, not the final fortress gates. There:

- packets misfiled under wrong seals;  
- analogies between tunnels that ignore structural difference;  
- example-lists of “successful advances” that do not justify universals;  
- records that look complete but answer the wrong question.

Handoff sentence for designers: **III settles who may authorize movement past the Court; IV tests what the deeper records can actually support.**

### Into Dungeon V — *Gates of Castrum Malae Ratiocinationis*
The Court is a front defense of the fortress: training travelers to obey finished-sounding speech. The Gates should feel like the same danger at full scale.

## 11. One-paragraph summary

The company of twenty reaches the Upper Archive’s Court of the Oracle: three institutional niches — Passage, Seal, and Charge — each with a sitting officer who speaks as if procedure itself were proof. The Oracles exist to issue final-sounding authorizations under incomplete records. Master of Ways, Keeper of Marks, and Advocate of the Bench disagree on jurisdiction but agree that a finished Court ruling ends inquiry. Their early tablets look decisive and remain problematic. Only late service ledgers and seal-cabinet history free the company to take the ugly **Records Service Corridor**, the clerks’ working route deeper into the Archive — best supported, not ceremonially blessed, and not the end of the fortress road.
