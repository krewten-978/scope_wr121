## Grading Vocabulary

- **Follow-test:** Decide whether the stated reason, together with any defensible hidden assumption, actually supports the conclusion.
- **Stable-term test:** Decide whether a key word keeps the same meaning throughout an argument.
- **Scope test:** Decide whether a claim's quantity—All, None, Some, or Most—is no broader than the supplied evidence supports.
- **Repair:** Revise a claim by stabilizing a term, narrowing its scope, correcting the inference, or identifying needed support without abandoning the original subject matter.
- **Carry-forward reasoning:** Score an earlier error where it occurs. In the final synthesis, still award credit when a student reasons consistently from the answer they recorded and uses the remaining evidence accurately; do not deduct twice for one misconception.

## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Gate of Applause four-step diagnosis (3 points)

**Expected Answer:**
- **Conclusion:** The Laurel Tunnel is the wisest route for the company.
- **Stated reason:** Travelers have applauded it for a hundred years.
- **Hidden assumption:** Long or widespread approval proves that a route is wise (or safe/best).
- **Follows:** No. Applause establishes approval or reputation, not wisdom or safety.

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Correct conclusion and reason; defensible hidden assumption; correct follow-test with explanation
- 2 pts: Correct judgment and most parts, but one element is vague or missing
- 1 pt: Recognizes that applause does not prove wisdom but does not parse the argument
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:** A precise paraphrase is acceptable. Do not require the word “popularity.”

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Narrow conclusion and needed evidence (2 points)

**Expected Answer:** A supported rewrite is: “Travelers have approved or applauded the Laurel Tunnel for a hundred years.” Relevant additional evidence could include direct inspection, recent safety records, reliable testimony from travelers who completed the route, or structural evidence.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Narrows the conclusion to approval/reputation and names relevant evidence
- 1 pt: Correct repair or relevant evidence, but not both
- 0 pts: Keeps the unsupported wisdom/safety conclusion or gives irrelevant evidence

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Stable-term rewrite and retest (2 points)

**Expected Answer:** One acceptable rewrite is: “The first chamber of the Echo passage is structurally sound because the masons found no cracks there. Therefore its first chamber has no observed cracks.” This limited conclusion follows from the supplied report. A rewrite claiming that the entire passage is structurally sound or that choosing it is wise does not follow because only the first chamber was examined.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Uses one stable meaning, limits the domain to the first chamber, and accurately retests the conclusion
- 1 pt: Stabilizes “sound” but retains an overbroad conclusion, or gives a good retest with an incomplete rewrite
- 0 pts: Preserves the meaning shift or claims the whole route is proved safe

**Notes for grading:** Other stable rewrites are acceptable if the student explicitly states whether the revised conclusion follows.

## Question 6: Part 3 Q2 – Ledger scope and repair (3 points)

**Expected Answer:**
- Claim A is supported by the sample: both recorded ash moths are winged. It applies only to the observed ash moths.
- Claim B is contradicted: one observed cave beetle is winged.
- Claim C is supported: at least one observed glass ant is winged.
- Claim D reaches beyond the evidence. The ledger is a sample from one chamber and cannot establish what most creatures in the entire Hall are like. Even within the sample, four of eight are winged, which is not more than half.
- Acceptable repairs include “Some creatures observed in the first chamber are winged,” “Some observed cave beetles are not winged,” or “Some observed glass ants are winged.”

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Correctly identifies B and D as unsafe for distinct reasons and supplies an evidence-sized repair
- 2 pts: Correct judgments with incomplete explanation, or one missed unsafe claim with a sound repair
- 1 pt: Recognizes sample limits or supplies a defensible repair but not both
- 0 pts: Treats absence from the ledger as proof about the entire Hall or gives no valid repair

**Notes for grading:** Do not accept “No cave beetles are winged.” “Some observed cave beetles are not winged” is supported.

## Question 9: Part 5 Q1 – Mapmaker follow-test and limits of logic (3 points)

**Expected Answer:**
- **Conclusion:** The map gives the true present condition of all three tunnels.
- **Stated reason:** The mapmaker copied every line carefully from an older map.
- **Hidden assumption:** An accurate copy of an undated, unverified older map accurately describes every tunnel today.
- **Follows:** No.
- Logic can expose the unsupported bridge from careful copying to present truth. Logic alone cannot establish the old map's accuracy, date, maker, the mapmaker's honesty, or current tunnel conditions. Those require inspection, testimony, provenance, or corroboration. The map is unverified, not thereby proved false.

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Complete four-step diagnosis and accurate logic/evidence boundary, without treating unverified as false
- 2 pts: Correct follow-test and boundary but one argument part is incomplete
- 1 pt: Says more evidence is needed but gives little logical analysis
- 0 pts: Accepts the conclusion or claims lack of proof makes the map false

## Question 10: Part 6 Q1 – Integrated claim repairs (2 points)

- (b) Example: “Some creatures recorded in the first-chamber sample are winged.” This narrows both the quantity and the evidence domain.

**Rubric:**

- 1 pt: One sound repair, or two repairs with one remaining overreach
- 0 pts: Both claims remain unsupported

## Question 11: Part 7 Q1 – Supported route synthesis (3 points)

**Expected Answer:** **Slate** is the best-supported next cautious step, not a guarantee of complete safety. One square-marked door was directly observed to rise toward daylight, and Part 4 established that the inscription supports at least some square doors opening upward. Laurel rests on applause rather than route evidence. Echo has evidence only about the first chamber's lack of observed cracks, and the guide's recommendation depended on a shift in “sound.” It remains unknown whether any tunnel is safe all the way to its exit. The most useful next observation would be direct inspection farther along the chosen Slate route, with a retreat plan, or comparable complete-route evidence for all options.

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Chooses Slate as the best-supported cautious step; accurately cites at least two stations; rejects one route for a logical reason; states uncertainty; proposes relevant next evidence
- 2 pts: Defensible synthesis with one required component missing, or a different route justified coherently from recorded earlier answers under the carry-forward rule
- 1 pt: Route choice with only one relevant reason or little treatment of uncertainty
- 0 pts: Unsupported guess or invented evidence

**Notes for grading:** Apply carry-forward reasoning. A student who made an earlier mistake may still earn synthesis credit for coherent use of their recorded result and accurate handling of the other evidence. No route is proved completely safe.
