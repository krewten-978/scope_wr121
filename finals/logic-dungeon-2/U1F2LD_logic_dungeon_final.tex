\textbf{Purpose:} Use the logic tools from Weeks 4--6 to select the best-supported route through the labyrinth.
\section*{The Second Seal}
Beyond the Hall of Inscriptions, twenty adventurers reach a circular chamber. The next seal lies in the \textbf{Upper Archive}. Three routes leave the chamber:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=0.08em]
\item \textbf{Raven Stair} --- an upward arrow is cut beneath an old raven seal.
\item \textbf{Well Passage} --- cold air flows through a round stone opening.
\item \textbf{Lantern Gallery} --- a blue survey thread disappears around its first turn.
\end{itemize}
No route may be accepted merely because it looks promising. At each station, the company must make a limited decision: record a clue, mark a route provisional, send a scout, avoid a trap, or repair a mistake.

\cluebox{\textbf{Two-test rule:} First apply the \emph{form test}: if the premises were granted, would the conclusion follow? Then apply the \emph{fact test}: are the premises established, doubtful, or false? A route can be best supported without being guaranteed safe.}

\textbf{Tools:} premises and conclusion; major, minor, and middle terms; practical syllogism map; A/E/I/O mood; figure; undistributed middle; two negative premises; validity; premise pressure point; soundness.

\section*{Part 4: The Fork of False Bridges}
The two Lantern scouts reach a fork. One path descends toward standing water; the other rises beside a narrow ledge. Two warnings are carved there.

\textbf{Flooded-fork warning:}
\cluebox{No flooded passages are dry passages.\\
No ledge routes are flooded passages.\\
Therefore, all ledge routes are dry passages.}

\textbf{Part 4 Q1:} State the mood and test the form. Explain what two negative premises fail to connect. What trap decision should the scouts make about assuming the ledge is dry?
\answerbox{Part 4 Q1 ANSWER BOX}{1.75in}

\textbf{Rising-path warning:}
\cluebox{All routes to the Upper Archive rise.\\
This ledge route rises.\\
Therefore, this ledge route goes to the Upper Archive.}

\textbf{Part 4 Q2:} Identify the figure and form error. Explain why a true conclusion would still not make this good proof, and state the scouts' justified next action.
\answerbox{Part 4 Q2 ANSWER BOX}{1.65in}
