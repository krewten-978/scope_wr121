## Grading Vocabulary for This Key

- **Equivocation:** one word used in two senses inside one argument.
- **Ambiguous middle:** the middle term looks shared but changes idea between premises.
- **Sense A / Sense B:** the two ordinary meanings of the shifting term.
- **Fixed-meaning rewrite:** restating the argument with one sense held steady.
- **Form test:** if the premises were true under one stable reading, would the conclusion follow?
- **Fact test:** are the premises true, false, doubtful, or unproved?
- **Fallacy autopsy:** conclusion, apparent proof, defect (Week 7 review).

## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Bats (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Shifting term: **bats**. Sense A: flying mammal / animal. Sense B: baseball (or sports) equipment. Conclusion illegitimately joins the two.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term + two distinct ordinary senses
- 1 pt: Term only or one weak sense
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Fair (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Shifting term: **fair** / **fairs**. Sense A: outdoor festival / county fair with vendors. Sense B: just, equitable, correct (a fair call). Conclusion fails once senses are fixed.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Term + both senses
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**
- Accept “festival/event” vs “just/equitable.”

- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

## Question 4: Part 3 Q1 – Present (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
**Present** shifts: (1) in attendance / here now; (2) gift. Fixed rewrite shows the gift is not a person in attendance, so “the gift should vote now” does **not** follow.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Clear fixed rewrite + original conclusion does not follow
- 1 pt: Senses only without retest
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing
