## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Championship from three wins (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** We will definitely win the championship (or equivalent).  
**Stated reason:** Our team has won its last three games (or equivalent).

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Correctly identifies both the conclusion and the stated reason in own words
- 0 pts: Incorrect, missing, or swaps conclusion and reason

**Notes for grading:**  
- Do not require hidden assumption or “follows” on this item.  
- Accept minor wording changes if the logical roles are clear.

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Sincere apology and regret (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** She must truly regret what she did (or equivalent).  
**Stated reason:** She apologized in a sincere tone (or equivalent).

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Correctly identifies both the conclusion and the stated reason
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Tone of apology is the stated support; regret is the claim.

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Study app downloads (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** The app must be the best tool for learning chemistry.  
**Stated reason:** It has been downloaded five million times.  
**Hidden assumption:** Download count or popularity is a reliable measure of educational quality / “best for learning.”  
**Follows:** No — popularity does not prove the app is the best learning tool.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: All four elements correct; “follows” correctly judged as no (or equivalent)
- 1 pt: Three elements correct or correct elements with weak assumption/follows explanation
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing most elements

**Notes for grading:**  
- Full credit requires labeling all four parts the lab asks for.

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Saturday rain and Friday concert (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** We should call off Friday night's outdoor concert.  
**Stated reason:** The weather report predicts rain on Saturday.  
**Hidden assumption:** Rain on Saturday is relevant to canceling Friday's event (or the forecast is accurate enough to drive the decision).  
**Follows:** No — the stated reason concerns a different day; the link to Friday is weak or missing.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Four elements present; follows correctly judged no because of day mismatch or weak connection
- 1 pt: Identifies conclusion and reason but weak assumption/follows
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Reward students who notice the Friday/Saturday gap.

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Unanimous council vote and fairness (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** The proposal must be fair.  
**Stated reason:** Every member of the student council voted for it.  
**Hidden assumption:** Unanimous approval by the student council guarantees fairness (or majority agreement = fairness).  
**Follows:** No — agreement within one group does not prove fairness.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: All four elements; follows = no with clear assumption about fairness and votes
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Accept equivalent phrasing for the unanimity → fairness assumption.

## Question 6: Part 3 Q2 – Most sources read and correctness (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** Jordan's conclusion must be correct.  
**Stated reason:** No one in the debate club has read more sources than Jordan.  
**Hidden assumption:** Reading the most sources guarantees a correct conclusion (or quantity of reading = correctness).  
**Follows:** No — research volume does not guarantee truth.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: All four elements correct; follows = no
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing

**Notes for grading:**  
- Distinguish from Part 3 Q1; assumption is about research quantity, not group vote.

## Question 7: Part 4 Q1 – Bus service paragraph (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**  
**Conclusion:** Late-night bus service should be widened across the east side (accept close paraphrase of the council’s final decision).  
**Stated reason:** Workers on evening shifts often cannot get home safely; ridership on the pilot route rose after the trial began (both reasons should appear or be summarized).  
**Hidden assumption:** Safety need and successful pilot ridership justify expanding service on the east side (or that the pilot results apply to the proposed expansion).  
**Follows:** No or only partly — reasons support expanded late-night service in general, but students may note the east-side scope is not fully proved by the reasons given; distractors (protesters, Ortiz’s brother, budget charts, scooter joke) should not be treated as the main reasons.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Correct conclusion and stated reasons drawn from the supporter lines; reasonable hidden assumption; follows judged with awareness of distractors (full credit for “no” or “partly” with brief justification)
- 1 pt: Finds main conclusion and at least one real reason but treats a distractor as key support, or weak assumption/follows
- 0 pts: Incorrect, missing, or mostly summarizes scene details without parsing the argument

**Notes for grading:**  
- **Distractors:** crowded hall, protesters, reporter, Ortiz’s brother anecdote, spending charts, scooter joke.  
- **Core argument** is in the supporters’ claims and the final council decision sentence.  
- Accept “partly follows” if the student explains that safety and pilot data support expansion but not necessarily only the east side.

**Overall Notes for Grading This Lab:**  
- Reward accurate logical roles over polished prose.  
- Whole points only (1, 2, 0). No half points.  
- Total: Part 1 Q1 (1) + Part 1 Q2 (1) + Part 2 Q1 (2) + Part 2 Q2 (2) + Part 3 Q1 (2) + Part 3 Q2 (2) + Part 4 Q1 (2) = 12 points.
