## Grading Vocabulary for This Key

- **Disciplined thinker:** one who repeatedly clarifies terms and claims, tests inference, weighs evidence and sources, surfaces assumptions, distinguishes rhetoric from proof, and revises when reasons require it.
- **Controlling claim:** the precise thesis the defense seeks to establish.
- **Portfolio evidence:** a specific artifact and recoverable feature used to support a growth claim.
- **Commentary:** explanation of how an artifact supports the thesis.
- **Warrant:** the principle connecting artifact evidence to the conclusion about growth.
- **Counterpressure:** a fair objection, contrary artifact, or remaining weakness that tests the thesis.
- **Qualification:** a limit that makes the thesis accurate and defensible.
- **Transfer claim:** a concrete action using a logical habit in a setting beyond the course.

Personalized evidence is required. Grade the quality of the argument and its verifiable artifact references; no single life story or portfolio selection is required.

## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Define disciplined thinking (3 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Defines disciplined thinking through at least three accurate, observable Whately habits and acknowledges a limit: logic does not supply facts, guarantee wisdom or good motives, or replace domain/moral judgment. A list without a coherent definition is partial.

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Coherent definition, three accurate habits, and a real limit
- 2 pts: Strong definition missing one requirement
- 1 pt: General care/effort language with little course precision
- 0 pts: Circular or inaccurate definition

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Controlling claim (3 points)

**Expected Answer:**
States a specific, supportable pattern of growth, such as increasingly proportionate claims, more stable terms, more explicit assumptions, better source testing, or stronger distinction between force and proof. It avoids claims of perfection and mere completion.

**Rubric:**
- 3 pts: Precise, arguable, portfolio-testable growth thesis
- 2 pts: Defensible but broad thesis
- 1 pt: Completion/effort claim with little reasoning focus
- 0 pts: No controlling claim
