## Grading Vocabulary

- **Fallacy:** reasoning that seems to prove a conclusion but fails to prove it.
- **Appearance of proof:** surface look of support (confidence, moral pressure, official form, restatement) without earned proof.
- **False claim:** an untrue or grossly unsupported statement used as if established.
- **Bad inference:** a leap from reasons to conclusion that does not follow, even if some claims are true.
- **Fallacy autopsy:** conclusion; apparent proof; defect; repair or narrowed claim.
- **Ambiguous middle / shifting term:** a key word that changes meaning between uses so the argument only appears to connect.
- **Fix-and-retest:** rewrite with one stable meaning and ask whether the original conclusion still follows.
- **Circular reasoning / begging the question:** the reason assumes the conclusion or cannot be accepted without already granting it.
- **Loaded definition:** a definition that smuggles the verdict into the meaning of a key term.
- **Independent support:** a reason that could be accepted without already accepting the conclusion.
- **Route consequence:** what the expedition may record, investigate, refuse, or risk after the diagnosis.
- **Provisional / not yet proved:** unfinished authorization; not the same as proved false.
- **Best-supported:** strongest conclusion under the supplied evidence; not a guarantee of total safety.
- **Carry-forward reasoning:** score an early error once; in later synthesis, award credit for coherent use of the student's recorded result while handling other evidence accurately.
