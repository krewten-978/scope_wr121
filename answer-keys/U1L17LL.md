## Grading Vocabulary for This Key

- **Self-deception:** persuading yourself of what you already want to believe.
- **Motivated reasoning:** desire chooses the conclusion first; reasons follow.
- **Rationalization:** reasons invented after the choice to protect the self.
- **Private argument / public cover:** inner justification versus cleaner outer story.
- **Hidden assumption:** unstated premise the argument needs.
- **Self-deception autopsy:** desire; conclusion; stated reasons; hidden assumption; exposing tool; remaining need; repaired narrower claim.
- **Review terms:** Week 16 rhetoric-vs-proof; Week 15 real dispute after shared definition.

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Group project method (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Hidden assumption: hours invested prove comparative quality, or sunk effort entitles method-dominance. Exposing tools: missing-the-point, weak induction, form-vs-fact, or hidden-assumption test—time spent does not entail “best method.”

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Surfaces assumption and names a fitting tool
- 1 pt: One element only
- 0 pts: Agrees the method is best because of hours

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Shortcut friend (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Weak move: loyalty/ethos of friendship treated as proof of safety expertise; possible irrelevant authority. Credibility/source tool: access, relevant expertise, bias, independent corroboration—friendship is not safety evidence.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names weak move and credibility/proof test
- 1 pt: Partial
- 0 pts: Accepts friend loyalty as safety proof

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Club title (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Private argument: I want the title for applications/status. Public cover: continuity for the club. Independent support needed for the cover: evidence the club actually needs this person for continuity, that alternatives are weaker, and that continuity—not résumé—is the governing aim.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Separates private/public and names needed support
- 1 pt: Partial separation
- 0 pts: Treats public cover as automatically true

## Question 6: Part 3 Q2 – Texting driver (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Rationalization: shifts blame to “anyone would” and to stoplights, protecting the self from responsibility. Repaired claim: I looked down at my phone while driving; that increased risk—regardless of stoplight design.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names rationalization and a fact-closer repaired claim
- 1 pt: One element only
- 0 pts: Excuses the driver fully

## Question 7: Part 4 Q1 – Play audition autopsy (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Desire: win the lead. Conclusion: I should get the part / if not, process was biased. Stated reasons: director likes confidence; nerves show deeper care; hard workers are rewarded; I work hard “in my head.” Hidden assumption: caring or private mental effort entitles the lead; outcome unfairness is default if denied. Exposing tools: circular/motivated reasoning, weak evidence, missing-the-point, equivocation on “hard work.” Still needs: actual audition criteria, comparative performance, director standards. Repaired claim: I want the lead and felt nervous because I care; that does not by itself prove I earned the part.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Completes a coherent multi-part autopsy
- 1 pt: Several elements but incomplete
- 0 pts: Only cheers or scolds Jordan

## Question 8: Part 5 Q1 – Review: campaign video (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**
Rhetorical force: pathos (music, torn flag) and ethos (“one of you”) pushing distrust/rejection. Unproved: the budget report’s content, whether rejection is justified, and whether identity belonging answers the policy question.

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Separates force from unproved policy claim
- 0 pts: Treats the video as proof
