## Grading Vocabulary for This Key

- **Discovery:** finding or learning a fact not yet known.
- **Proof:** showing that a conclusion follows from given reasons or evidence.
- **Observation:** direct attention to facts, events, measurements, records, or evidence.
- **Testimony:** information received from another source or witness.
- **Imagination / hypothesis:** a possible explanation proposed for testing; useful for inquiry but not proof by itself.
- **Memory:** retained knowledge that can suggest a pattern but may need confirmation.
- **Judgment:** deciding which logic tool is needed and how strongly the evidence supports the conclusion.
- **Intellectual-tool map:** question or problem; needed discovery; clarified terms or claims; inference/proof test; judgment about relevance, probability, or application.
- **Review terms:** prior-week source test (authority, relevant evidence) and induction test (examples, overclaim, revised claim).

## Question 1: Part 1 Q1 – Gym roof leak (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
The leak must still be discovered by observation, records, inspection, maintenance reports, or testimony from people who saw water. Reasoning can then organize the evidence and test a conclusion such as whether this roof leaks, where it leaks, or whether age is a likely cause. A general argument about old roofs does not by itself prove this roof leaks.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Separates needed discovery from later reasoning/proof
- 1 pt: Says “check the roof” but gives little distinction
- 0 pts: Treats the general argument as proof of the fact

## Question 2: Part 1 Q2 – Deadline pattern (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Discovery: the team noticed a repeated pattern among missed deadlines this month. Inference: unclear instructions may be part of the problem. The conclusion is sized carefully because it says “may be part,” not “always causes” or “is the only cause.”

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names discovery, inference, and careful claim size
- 1 pt: Identifies the pattern but not the claim size
- 0 pts: Treats the pattern as a universal proof or misses the inference

## Question 3: Part 2 Q1 – Fairness dispute (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
The first needed tool-work is term clarification because “fair” is being used in two different senses: same rule for everyone versus treatment adjusted to different needs. Until the meaning is clarified, the sides may be partly in a verbal dispute and cannot judge the real policy disagreement clearly.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Identifies term clarification/verbal-dispute work and explains why
- 1 pt: Says define “fair” without explaining the two senses
- 0 pts: Immediately chooses a side without clarification

## Question 4: Part 2 Q2 – Fundraiser expenses (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
The group must discover expenses as well as revenue, probably by checking records, receipts, or net profit. After that, the proof question is whether the fundraiser truly succeeded by the relevant standard: more gross money, more net money, better participation, or another clarified goal.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Names needed financial discovery and the remaining proof/standard question
- 1 pt: Mentions expenses only
- 0 pts: Accepts higher revenue alone as settled success

## Question 5: Part 3 Q1 – Plant hypothesis (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
The light explanation is a useful hypothesis produced by imagination, but it is not proof until tested. The group needs observation or method: compare similar plants in window and non-window locations, control watering/plant type, measure growth, and check whether light rather than another factor explains the difference.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Distinguishes hypothesis from proof and names a real observation/method
- 1 pt: Says “test it” vaguely
- 0 pts: Treats the imagined explanation as proven

## Question 6: Part 3 Q2 – Library memory (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Memory supplies a relevant prior case or starting expectation, but it does not settle this year's situation. Stronger judgment would come from current observation, library schedule, librarian testimony, exam-week policy, room-reservation records, or recent experience from this year.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Explains memory's role and gives relevant confirming evidence
- 1 pt: Mentions checking this year but not memory's limited role
- 0 pts: Treats last year's memory as certainty

## Question 7: Part 4 Q1 – Bike-lane tool map (2 points)

**Expected Answer:**
Question: did the bike lane make streets safer, and should it stay? Needed discovery: compare police crash reports, hospital injury records, traffic-speed data, and definitions/counting methods before and after the lane. Clarification: “safer,” “crashes dropped,” “traffic is worse,” and whether success means fewer crashes, fewer injuries, lower speeds, or acceptable tradeoffs. Inference test: whether partial reports from the cycling group or two driver trips support broad conclusions. Judgment: weigh broader evidence, avoid one-sided samples, and decide whether the safety evidence supports keeping, changing, or further studying the lane.

**Rubric:**
- 2 pts: Includes question, needed discovery, clarification, inference test, and judgment
- 1 pt: Includes some map elements but incomplete
- 0 pts: Accepts one side without Week 14 analysis

## Question 8: Part 5 Q1 – Review: athlete app authority (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**
The argument uses celebrity or endorsement authority from a famous athlete. More relevant evidence would include independent reviews, learning data, teacher/student results, privacy/security checks if relevant, or expertise from educators or study-design researchers.

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Identifies weak authority and more relevant evidence
- 0 pts: Treats fame as enough proof

## Question 9: Part 5 Q2 – Review: flashcards overclaim (1 point)

**Expected Answer:**
Examples: four students improved after using flashcards. Overclaim: flashcards guarantee higher grades for every student. Revised claim: flashcards may help some students improve, or they are worth trying/testing with more students and subjects.

**Rubric:**
- 1 pt: Names examples, overclaim, and narrowed revised claim
- 0 pts: Incorrect or missing
